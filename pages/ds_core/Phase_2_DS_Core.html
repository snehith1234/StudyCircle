Reality check

Data scientists spend ~80% of their time on steps 2–4 (cleaning, exploring, engineering). The modeling step is often the smallest part. Good data prep matters more than a fancy algorithm.

3. Feature Engineering

Data Leakage Warning

Always fit scalers/encoders on training data only, then transform both train and test. Fitting on the full dataset leaks test information into training — your metrics will be overly optimistic and the model will fail in production.

4. Exploratory Data Analysis

The EDA Mindset

EDA isn't a checklist you rush through — it's where you build intuition about the data. Every plot should answer a question. If a plot doesn't tell you something new, it's not worth making. Focus on: what's the target distribution? Which features correlate with the target? Are there surprising patterns or data quality issues?
