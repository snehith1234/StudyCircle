# Career Transition Assessment: QA Testing → Agentic AI

## Overall Chances: **Good to Very Good (65-80%)**

---

## Factors Working FOR You

### 1. Perfect Timing (Market)
- Agentic AI is exploding right now (2025-2026)
- Demand far exceeds supply of qualified engineers
- Companies are hiring people with adjacent skills and training them
- The field is so new that nobody has "10 years of Agentic AI experience"

### 2. Your QA Background is Actually Valuable
- AI reliability/testing is a massive unsolved problem
- Companies desperately need people who can evaluate AI systems
- Your edge case thinking transfers directly to prompt engineering
- "AI Quality Engineer" is an emerging role with few candidates

### 3. You're Already Taking Action
- You have a structured learning plan
- You're building projects (real hands-on work)
- You're thinking about interviews early (smart preparation)

---

## Factors Working AGAINST You (Be Honest)

### 1. Competition
- Fresh ML/CS graduates flooding the market
- People with PhD backgrounds targeting same roles
- Bootcamp graduates with more recent training

### 2. Knowledge Gap
- Statistics → Agentic AI is a long journey (~11 months)
- Deep learning fundamentals take time to internalize
- You'll be competing with people who've done this for years

### 3. Age/Experience Perception
- Some companies prefer "moldable" junior candidates
- 10 years in QA might make some recruiters assume you're "stuck"

## How to Maximize Your Chances

### High Impact Actions
1. **Build in public** - GitHub projects, LinkedIn posts, blog
2. **Target AI + QA intersection** - Less competition, unique value
